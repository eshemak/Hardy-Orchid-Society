Membership

Joining the Hardy Orchid Society connects you with a knowledgeable, supportive community dedicated to hardy and wild orchids. Members gain access to expert learning, practical experience, and opportunities to contribute to orchid conservation and research.

Meetings

Three indoor meetings each year featuring specialist talks, plant sales, competitions, and opportunities to meet fellow enthusiasts.

Meetings

Guided Field Trips

Explore orchid sites across the UK during peak flowering season with expert guidance.

Guided Field Trips

Quarterly Journal

A full-colour publication featuring cultivation advice, taxonomy updates, field reports, and research findings.

Quarterly Journal

Seed Bank Access

Members can obtain seed from cultivated hardy orchids and learn propagation techniques.

Seed Bank Access

Hands-On Workshops

Practical training events covering sowing, potting, and specialist growing conditions.

Hands-On Workshops

Photography & Plant Shows

Annual competitions and displays, including the RHS Banksian Medal.

Photography & Plant Shows

Members’ Discussion Forum

A friendly, moderated space for advice, identification help, and sharing observations.

Members’ Discussion Forum

Conservation Support

Guidance from the HOS Conservation Officer and opportunities to assist with rescue operations.

Conservation Support

Research Opportunities

Members can contribute to scientific studies in taxonomy, pollination biology, and long-term monitoring.

Research Opportunities
