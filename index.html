The Hardy Orchid Society brings together people who share a passion for the wild and native orchids of Britain, Europe and other temperate regions around the world. Whether you are an experienced botanist or someone who has only recently discovered these remarkable plants, HOS offers a warm community and a rich source of knowledge for anyone interested in hardy orchids.

About Us

Our mission is to promote the understanding, enjoyment and conservation of hardy orchids in the wild and in cultivation. Members benefit from shared expertise, hands-on learning and opportunities to contribute to meaningful conservation and research efforts.

What We Do

Members enjoy a wide range of activities designed to deepen knowledge, build community, and support orchid conservation:

Regular Meetings

Bring enthusiasts together to learn and connect

Quarterly Journal

Features articles, research, and cultivation insights

Field Visits

Visit orchid sites across the UK to see species in their natural habitats

Plant & Seed Access

Cultivated orchid plants and seeds through members' sales and the seed bank

Practical Workshops

Learn hands-on techniques including seed sowing

Plant Show

Celebrate the diversity of hardy orchids

Photography Competition

Showcase members' creativity and fieldwork

Discussion Forum

Web-based platform for sharing advice and discoveries

Get Involved

Joining the Hardy Orchid Society opens the door to a vibrant community of orchid lovers. Whether you want to improve your cultivation skills, explore orchid habitats or contribute to conservation and research, there's a place for you here.

  • Learn from experienced cultivators and botanists
  • Discover rare orchid species and their habitats
  • Contribute to conservation efforts
  • Share your passion with like-minded enthusiasts
  • Access exclusive resources and seed collections
