Membership

Joining the Hardy Orchid Society connects you with a knowledgeable, supportive community dedicated to hardy and wild orchids. Members gain access to expert learning, practical experience, and opportunities to contribute to orchid conservation and research.

Meetings

Three indoor meetings each year featuring specialist talks, plant sales, competitions, and opportunities to meet fellow enthusiasts.

View Meetings

Guided Field Trips

Explore orchid sites across the UK during peak flowering season. Experienced members help you identify species, understand habitats, and appreciate orchids in their natural environments.

Field Trip Details

Quarterly Journal

A high-quality, full-colour publication featuring cultivation advice, taxonomy updates, field reports, research findings, and outstanding photography.

Read the Journal

Seed Bank Access

Members can obtain seed from cultivated hardy orchids and learn propagation techniques through practical demonstrations and seed-sowing sessions.

Seed Bank Info

Hands-On Workshops

Practical training events that build essential skills for successful cultivation, including sowing, potting, and maintaining specialist growing conditions.

Photography & Plant Shows

Annual competitions and displays, including the prestigious RHS Banksian Medal awarded at the Spring Meeting.

Members’ Discussion Forum

A friendly, moderated online space for advice, identification help, cultivation tips, and sharing observations throughout the year.

Conservation Support

Guidance from the HOS Conservation Officer, opportunities to assist with rescue operations, and grants supporting orchid conservation projects.
