Field Trips 2026

Explore Britain’s orchids with experienced leaders and fellow enthusiasts.

Before You Book

HOS members are welcome to join any field trip, but you must register with the trip leader. Many trips fill quickly — early booking is recommended.

Important: You are responsible for your own safety. Conditions can be uneven, steep or weather‑affected. Prepare as for a countryside walk.

Register by emailing the trip leader (addresses in the Spring 2026 Journal). For general enquiries: fieldtrips@hardyorchidsociety.org

Membership required: You must be a member of the Hardy Orchid Society to book. Join the Society

2026 Field Trip Programme

Tuesday 26 May — East Kent
Leader: Colin Sillence • Sites: Parkgate & Denge Wood Fully booked

A classic Kent orchid day: Monkey, Lady, Fly and Greater Butterfly orchids, with up to twelve species possible. Easy walking; car sharing required due to limited parking.

Thursday 28 May — Warburg Reserve, Henley‑on‑Thames
Leader: Keith Boseley Fully booked

A rich Chilterns reserve with Fly, Bird’s‑nest, Early Purple and Greater Butterfly orchids. Some steep inclines. Guided by a long‑term volunteer.

Saturday 30 May — Box Hill, Surrey
Leader: Richard Kulczycki

A strenuous 10‑mile downland walk with around ten orchid species. Loop route starting near Box Hill & West Humble station. Option to join later for Brockham Quarry only.

Thursday 4 June — Folkestone & East Kent
Leader: Ian Hadingham Fully booked

Multi‑site day covering Ophrys fuciflora, Orchis purpurea, Ophrys apifera, Himantoglossum hircinum and more. Significant driving required.

Saturday 13 June — Braunton Burrows, North Devon
Leader: Roger Harding

A three‑hour walk through one of Britain’s finest dune systems. Early summer orchids include Early Marsh, Southern Marsh, Bee, Pyramidal and Twayblade.

Sunday 14 June — Waitby Greenriggs, Cumbria
Leader: Charlie Philpotts

One of Britain’s best orchid reserves. Fly Orchids should be at their peak. Date may shift depending on flowering.

Saturday 20 June — Three Sites near Leeds
Leader: Charlie Philpotts

Three contrasting sites with Northern Marsh‑orchid, Southern Marsh‑orchid, Bee Orchid, Twayblade and hybrids.

Saturday 20 June — Windsor Hill
Registration: fieldtrips@hardyorchidsociety.org

To see Red Helleborine. Viewing from an enclosure; flowering varies year to year.

Sunday 21 June — Kenfig Dunes, Glamorgan
Leader: Steve Parsons Fully booked

Focus on Fen Orchid, plus high‑summer species. Guided by the site warden.

Saturday 27 June — Ladle Hill, Hampshire Downs
Leader: Simon Melville Fully booked

Burnt Orchids, Frog Orchids, Fragrant‑orchids and chalk grassland flora. Easy 25‑minute walk.

Saturday 4 July — Noar Hill, North Hampshire
Leaders: Ken & Gillian Elsom Waiting list only

A diverse medieval chalk site with Musk Orchid, Fragrant‑orchid, Fly Orchid and more.

Sunday 12 July — Waitby Greenriggs & Augill Pasture
