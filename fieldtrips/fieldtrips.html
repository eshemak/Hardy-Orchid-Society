Field Trips 2026

Explore Britain’s orchids with experienced leaders and fellow enthusiasts.

Before You Book

HOS members are welcome to join any field trip, but you must register with the trip leader. Many trips fill quickly — early booking is recommended.

Important: You are responsible for your own safety. Conditions can be uneven, steep or weather‑affected. Prepare as for a countryside walk.

Register by emailing the trip leader (addresses in the Spring 2026 Journal). For general enquiries: fieldtrips@hardyorchidsociety.org

Membership required: You must be a member of the Hardy Orchid Society to book. Join the Society

2026 Field Trip Programme

Tuesday 26th May — East Kent
Leader: Colin Sillence Fully booked

A classic day in England’s orchid heartland. We will visit Parkgate and the famous Denge Wood “Bonsai Bank”, home to Monkey, Lady, Fly and Greater Butterfly Orchids. With favourable conditions, as many as twelve species may be seen. The route is under two miles and mostly easy going. Parking is extremely limited, so car sharing is essential. East Kent offers many additional orchid sites for those wishing to explore further.

Thursday 28th May — Warburg Reserve, Henley‑on‑Thames
Leader: Keith Boseley Fully booked

Warburg is a beautiful BBOWT reserve in the Chilterns, long known for its rich orchid flora. Although numbers have declined, the reserve still supports Fly, Bird’s‑nest, Early Purple and Greater Butterfly Orchids, along with Common Twayblade, Pyramidal and Common Spotted. Lesser Butterfly‑orchid has not been seen since 2022, but several helleborine species should be in leaf. Your leader has volunteered at Warburg for a decade and will guide the group to the best areas. The reserve includes steep slopes, so participants must be comfortable with uphill walking.

Saturday 30th May — Box Hill, Surrey
Leader: Richard Kulczycki

A long, rewarding downland walk across Box Hill and on to Brockham Quarry. Expect around ten orchid species along the route. This is a strenuous ten‑mile walk with several steep sections. We meet near Box Hill & West Humble station and complete a loop, so parking at the start is possible. Those preferring a shorter outing may join mid‑afternoon for the Brockham Quarry section only.

Thursday 4th June — Folkestone & East Kent Sites
Leader: Ian Hadingham Fully booked

A full and varied day exploring several of East Kent’s finest orchid sites. We begin with Ophrys fuciflora, then move on to see Orchis purpurea (including var. alba), possible Ophrys insectifera, Platanthera bifolia, Neottia nidus‑avis, and hundreds of Neottia ovata. At Monkton Nature Reserve we expect Orchis anthropophora (including var. alba), Dactylorhiza praetermissa and Ophrys apifera. We finish at Sandwich and Sandwich Bay, one of England’s best sites for Himantoglossum hircinum. This trip involves significant driving — please ensure you can navigate confidently by phone, SatNav or with a co‑navigator.

Saturday 13th June — Braunton Burrows, North Devon
Leader: Roger Harding

A three‑hour walk through one of Britain’s most important dune systems. The terrain is generally easy, though exposed to coastal weather, and some damp slacks may require waterproof footwear. Guided by John or Mary Breeds, who have decades of experience managing this exceptional reserve. Early summer orchids may include Early Marsh, Southern Marsh (including hybrids and coccinea forms), Bee Orchid, Pyramidal Orchid and Twayblade. Expect a wealth of other rare plants, insects and birds.

Sunday 14th June — Waitby Greenriggs, Cumbria
Leader: Charlie Philpotts

One of Britain’s finest orchid reserves, with numerous species flowering throughout the season. Fly Orchids are usually at their peak in early June, but the exact date may shift depending on flowering. Expect a range of other orchids and a rich supporting flora. A superb introduction to Cumbrian orchid country.

Saturday 20th June — Three Sites near Leeds
Leader: Charlie Philpotts

A varied day visiting three contrasting habitats: a former mining site, a limestone outcrop and an old limestone meadow. Orchids may include Northern Marsh, Southern Marsh, Common Spotted, their hybrids, Twayblade and Bee Orchid. This trip offers an excellent opportunity to compare Dactylorhiza species side by side.

Saturday 20th June — Windsor Hill, near Princes Risborough
Registration: fieldtrips@hardyorchidsociety.org

A special visit to see the rare Red Helleborine. The plants grow within an enclosure, so viewing is from a short distance, and flowering cannot be guaranteed — though recent years have been encouraging. The date may shift depending on the season. The walk is around one mile in total. BBOWT charges a per‑person fee for guided visits; donations will be collected to support their conservation work. The site warden will be present to explain the history and management of this remarkable reserve.

Sunday 21st June — Kenfig Dunes, Glamorgan
Leader: Steve Parsons Fully booked

A visit timed for the best chance of seeing the rare Fen Orchid, along with high‑summer species such as Early Marsh Orchid var. coccinea. Kenfig is a unique and celebrated dune system with a wealth of botanical interest. The site warden will guide us through the reserve. Expect a fair amount of walking over sandy terrain.

Saturday 27th June — Ladle Hill, Hampshire Downs
Leader: Simon Melville Fully booked

A beautiful chalk downland site with late‑flowering Burnt Orchid, Frog Orchid, Pyramidal Orchid (including possible f. emarginata), Common Spotted, Chalk Fragrant and Bee Orchid. Dactylorhiza × mixta has been recorded previously. The chalk flora is exceptionally rich, and the views across north Hampshire and west Berkshire are outstanding. The site also contains England’s only unfinished hillfort. A mostly flat 25‑minute walk from the parking area, though some uneven ground is present.

Saturday 4th July — Noar Hill, North Hampshire
Leaders: Ken & Gillian Elsom Fully booked — waiting list only

Noar Hill is a former medieval chalk working now transformed into a biodiversity hotspot. Expect Musk Orchid, Common Spotted, Pyramidal, Chalk Fragrant, Fly Orchid, Twayblade and — depending on the season — Violet Helleborine. The site also supports many butterflies and bird species. Some paths are steep and can be slippery when wet.

Sunday 12th July — Waitby Greenriggs & Augill Pasture, Cumbria
Leader: Charlie Philpotts

A two‑site day in the Kirkby Stephen area. Orchids may include Northern Marsh, Common Spotted, Heath Spotted, Greater and Lesser Butterfly Orchid, Marsh Helleborine and three varieties of Fragrant Orchid. Hybrids are frequent and fascinating. If time allows, we may visit a nearby site to look for Lesser Twayblade.

Saturday 18th July — Braunton Burrows, North Devon (Second Visit)
Leader: Roger Harding

A second visit to this exceptional dune system, following a different route and timed for later‑season orchids. Expect thousands of Marsh Helleborines, Marsh Fragrant Orchid, Pyramidal Orchid and Twayblade. Early and Southern Marsh Orchids may still be present, including hybrids and coccinea forms. Guided again by Mary Breeds. This later date also offers the chance to see the rare Water Germander and a wealth of other wildlife.
