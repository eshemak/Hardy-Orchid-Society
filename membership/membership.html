Membership

The Hardy Orchid Society supports everyone with an interest in hardy and wild orchids, offering members high-quality learning opportunities, practical experience, and a welcoming community.

Join the Society

Benefits of Joining the Hardy Orchid Society

Membership Benefits

  • Expert meetings: Three indoor events each year featuring specialist talks, plant sales, competitions, and time to meet and learn from other orchid enthusiasts.
  • Guided field trips: Visits to orchid sites across the UK during the flowering season, led by knowledgeable members who help you explore species in their natural habitats.
  • Quarterly journal: A full-colour publication with articles on cultivation, taxonomy, field studies, research updates, and photography.
  • Seed bank access: Members can obtain seed from cultivated hardy orchids and learn propagation techniques through practical sessions.
  • Hands-on workshops: Practical workshops such as seed-sowing sessions that teach essential skills for growing hardy orchids successfully.
  • Photography and plant shows: Annual competitions and displays, including the RHS Banksian Medal awarded at the Spring Meeting.
  • Members' discussion forum: A friendly, moderated online space for advice, identification help, cultivation tips, and sharing observations.
  • Conservation support: Guidance from the HOS Conservation Officer, involvement in rescue operations, and grants supporting orchid conservation projects.
  • Research opportunities: Opportunities to contribute to scientific studies on taxonomy, pollination biology, and long-term monitoring of orchid populations.

Why Join?

Membership in the Hardy Orchid Society connects you with a knowledgeable, supportive community while helping protect the UK's native orchids. Whether you are new to orchids or an experienced grower, the HOS provides the expertise, resources, and opportunities to deepen your understanding and enjoy orchids in both cultivation and the wild.
