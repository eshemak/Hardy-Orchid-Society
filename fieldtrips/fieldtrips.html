Field Trips 2026

Explore Britain’s orchids with experienced leaders and fellow enthusiasts.

Before You Book

HOS members are welcome to join any field trip, but you must register with the trip leader. Many trips fill quickly — early booking is recommended.

Important: You are responsible for your own safety. Conditions can be uneven, steep or weather‑affected. Prepare as for a countryside walk.

Register by emailing the trip leader (addresses in the Spring 2026 Journal). For general enquiries: fieldtrips@hardyorchidsociety.org

Membership required: You must be a member of the Hardy Orchid Society to book. Join the Society

2026 Field Trip Programme

Tuesday 26th May — East Kent
Leader: Colin Sillence Fully booked

A classic day in England’s orchid heartland. We will visit Parkgate and the famous Denge Wood “Bonsai Bank”, home to Monkey, Lady, Fly and Greater Butterfly Orchids. With favourable conditions, as many as twelve species may be seen. The route is under two miles and mostly easy going. Parking is extremely limited, so car sharing is essential. East Kent offers many additional orchid sites for those wishing to explore further.

Thursday 28th May — Warburg Reserve, Henley‑on‑Thames
Leader: Keith Boseley Fully booked

Warburg is a beautiful BBOWT reserve in the Chilterns, long known for its rich orchid flora. Although numbers have declined, the reserve still supports Fly, Bird’s‑nest, Early Purple and Greater Butterfly Orchids, along with Common Twayblade, Pyramidal and Common Spotted. Lesser Butterfly‑orchid has not been seen since 2022, but several helleborine species should be in leaf. Your leader has volunteered at Warburg for a decade and will guide the group to the best areas. The reserve includes steep slopes, so participants must be comfortable with uphill walking.

Saturday 30th May — Box Hill, Surrey
Leader: Richard Kulczycki

A long, rewarding downland walk across Box Hill and on to Brockham Quarry. Expect around ten orchid species along the route. This is a strenuous ten‑mile walk with several steep sections. We meet near Box Hill & West Humble station and complete a loop, so parking at the start is possible. Those preferring a shorter outing may join mid‑afternoon for the Brockham Quarry section only.

For all enquiries: fieldtrips@hardyorchidsociety.org
